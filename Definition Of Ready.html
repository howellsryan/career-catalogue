Definition of Ready

Requirements for work to be undertaken by the development team and brought into a sprint

Introduction

Our Definition of Ready represents the requirements for work to be undertaken by the development team and brought into a sprint. We should refine stories on a regular basis to bring them in line with this definition.

Key Criteria

Acceptance Criteria

Acceptance criteria is in place for the story.

Team Understanding

The intent of the story and the work involved is understood by the team and/or clearly documented.

Value Addition

The story represents adding value to the product or service.

Deliverable Outcome

Outcome of the story is something deliverable.

Proper Breakdown

The story is broken down into earliest deliverable chunk, as soon as there is value, we should split work up.

No Blockers

There are no known blockers that would prevent work from starting.

Refinement Process

Regular refinement sessions help ensure that stories are properly prepared before being considered for sprint planning. This increases team efficiency and delivery success.

  1. Initial Review: Product owner presents new stories to the team.
  2. Discussion: Team discusses requirements, technical approach, and potential challenges.
  3. Refinement: Stories are refined with acceptance criteria and any necessary documentation.
  4. Final Check: Team confirms that all Definition of Ready criteria are met before bringing stories to sprint planning.

Sprint Planning

Ready for Sprint Planning!

Once stories meet the Definition of Ready criteria, they can be brought to Sprint Planning where we can schedule and commit to undertaking the work.
